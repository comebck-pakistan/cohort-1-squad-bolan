# Invoice Dataset Analysis & OCR Research Findings

## 1. Purpose

This document summarizes the observations collected during invoice OCR feasibility testing.

The goal was to understand:

- Types of invoices used by Pakistani FMCG distributors
- Challenges faced by OCR systems
- Effect of image quality and handwriting
- Accuracy limitations
- Requirements for building a reliable invoice understanding pipeline

Dataset analyzed:

- 09 invoice samples
- Different suppliers
- Different formats
- Printed and handwritten invoices
- Urdu, English, and mixed-language documents

# 3. Key Research Findings


## Finding 1: Invoice Formats Are Highly Inconsistent

The dataset contains:

- handwritten bills
- printed distributor invoices
- cash memos
- ledger-style invoices
- corporate FMCG invoices

There is no single fixed format.

### Impact:

A rule-based extraction system will fail because:

- table positions change
- field names differ
- products appear differently
- totals are placed differently


### Recommendation:

Use a flexible document understanding pipeline instead of fixed templates.


---

# Finding 2: Handwritten Invoices Are the Biggest Challenge

Handwritten invoices caused the majority of OCR errors.

Common issues:

- unclear product names
- overwritten text
- crossed-out items
- Urdu handwriting variations
- missing totals
- incomplete words


Examples:

- INV001:
  - product names were difficult even for humans to read
  - OCR confused similar handwritten words

- INV008:
  - product catalog improved normalization
  - however, OCR still confused similar products


### Recommendation:

Do not rely only on OCR.

Pipeline should include:

Image OCR
+
Product Catalog Matching
+
Confidence Score
+
Human Verification for uncertain cases


---

# Finding 3: Product Catalog Improves Normalization

Without catalog:

Example:

OCR output:


Possible interpretations:

- Sweet
- Cake Rush
- Other similar products


With catalog:

The system can compare extracted text against known products.

Benefits:

- correct spelling mistakes
- handle Urdu variations
- normalize product names


### Recommendation:

Maintain a product catalog containing:

| Field | Example |
|-|-|
| Product ID | SKU001 |
| English Name | Cake Rush |
| Urdu Name | کیک رش |
| Brand | Bisconni |
| Common OCR Variations | کیک رش / کیک رش فیملی |


---

# Finding 4: Printed Invoices Perform Significantly Better

Printed invoices showed much higher accuracy.

Examples:

INV003, INV004, INV007, INV010

Advantages:

- clear tables
- fixed text
- readable numbers
- structured totals


OCR successfully extracted:

- invoice numbers
- customer details
- taxes
- discounts
- product rows


---

# Finding 5: Image Quality Directly Affects Accuracy

Problems observed:

- shadows
- folded papers
- background objects
- low contrast
- tilted images


Example:

INV001:

The invoice was difficult because:

- background objects were visible
- handwriting overlapped
- lighting was inconsistent


### Recommendation:

Before OCR:

Apply image preprocessing:

- crop invoice area
- remove noise
- improve contrast
- correct rotation
- enhance handwriting regions


---

# 4. OCR Testing Observations

## Prompt Evolution

Multiple prompts were tested.

### Early prompts:

Problems:

- model guessed missing values
- incomplete handwriting was completed incorrectly
- hallucinated product names


### Improved prompts:

Added instructions:

- do not guess
- return unreadable
- distinguish absent vs unreadable fields
- preserve visible text only


Result:

Improved reliability.

# 5. Current Limitations


## 1. Product Recognition

Problem:

Similar products create confusion.

Example:

Handwritten versions may look similar.

Solution:

Use:

- product catalog
- fuzzy matching
- confidence threshold
